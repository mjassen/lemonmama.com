High Level Athletes Stories Managing Money

Four stories about athletes managing money.

Athlete money story 1 of 4:

First is a money athlete story that was amazing for me to hear.

In this UFC Fight night show, the announcers mention in passing, that Persona John Castaneda (going up against Persona Muin Gafurov tonight) works a full time job besides his MMA training schedule.

2023 june 3 'UFC Fight Night"
https://www.ufc.com/event/ufc-fight-night-june-03-2023

Lemonmama note:
O. M. G. so inspiring.

And I admire Persona Castaneda and he inspires me for his work ethic and his personality and his smarts and his strength, from what I see so far.

But also I have so many questions about this.
Does this mean Persona Castaneda is, in terms of income coming in from the fighting, currently partly a hobbyist or an amateur?
What I mean by that is, does he's not (yet currently) earning enough direct money income from the prizes/pay from fighting to pay for all his life expenses currently?

Based only on what I heard from this show, it means that I don't know based on what i know so far, whether or not Persona Castaneda earns _anything_ income from fighting or not. 

In other words, if he fights, loses, or wins, is there any income money that he can get from that, or not? If so how much?

But it sounds like so far, that the income is very little or no income from the fighting.

simply amazing.

I get inspired and admire Persona Castaneda for his accomplishments.

But a question I have is the following; is the guy in a situation living paycheck to paycheck?

If the announcers say is true then the guy still works a job outside fighting, and I would assume he does in order to pay the bills, support life expenses, etc. etc.

amazing.
