Regarding Work Job Burnout




1.)
Regarding work job burnout.

I noticed thing.

- How burned out you are.
- And whether or not you are burned out.

In working class paycheck-to-paycheck jobs.

17 jobs over 30 years.

Colleagues and bosses.

Use these thing to judge how hard you (their colleague) is working.

Use these thing to judge whether or not you are working hard.



2.)
Further.

It's hard to see this if one is entrenched in the working class paycheck-to-paycheck lifestyle.

Still further.

This only works if everyone is always burned out.

This paradigm also promotes burnout.

Let me say this in other words.

Said otherwise, it doesn't work.

Because being burned out at work means you are also burned out in life.

Because the definition of burnout is it spirals and it can't be recovered in one weekend.

How to proceed?...



3.)
...How to proceed?

Find a good company with a good culture.

Scratch that.

First take care of yourself.

Starting from burned out state assuming.

Work part time, work less hours, repeat as necessary.

Work a job role with less tasks, less responsibilities, repeat as necessary.

Invite work colleagues to hang out outside of work. Before after work.

The ones who accept the hangout invite are allies.

However.

That doesn't fix things fully either.

So.

This is a work in progress.


Next. ...



4.)
...Next.

Take care of yourself.

Then proceed.

Find a good company with a good culture.

Continue to work hard. 

Hold oneself accountable.

Hold colleagues accountable.

Work more tasks, more responsibilities, repeat.

Now this time at a better company, better self, better colleagues.

Repeat.

After all, a wise person once said, for career job burnout, that "you own your burnout".



In conclusion, these were some thoughts regarding work job burnout, and, in that context some way how to proceed.
