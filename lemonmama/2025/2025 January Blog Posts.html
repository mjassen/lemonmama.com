#0076 - Massachusetts Man Discovers New Talent, Promotes Music Artist's Songs to His Friends

Morgan Jassen, a 43-year old white man who wears a beard, said.

Picture of Mr. Jassen here:



"I just had to know who was singing this."

According to Mr. Jassen he has a history of finding fresh new sounds, songs, and singers, even before anyone else, including his friends, have heard of them. 

To this point, Mr. Jassen told us:

"I hadn't been listening for new music for a while, then I turned on the radio and “Austin” by Dasha was playing on the number 22 slot of the countdown."

Mr. Jassen says he still remembers the first time he heard "Austin" by Dasha on the radio.

"I told my friend I discovered this new talented artist and they had to hear this song!"

When asked to comment, Mr. Jassen's friend said yeah, they heard the Dasha Song last year.
 
In our interview, still nodding to the beat and singing, Mr. Jassen made the following prediction: "I think this one could get to #1 on the charts, I bet you it will at least be higher on the charts next week.”

Finally, we asked Mr. Jassen what genres of music he likes to which he commented: 
“I like all different kinds of music, I listen to everything.”

#0072 - Wall Graph of Your Life 001


I made thing.

I made this budget chart called wall graph of your life.

I learned thing.

Two immediate notes, what I learned, doing this iteration of updating my wall graph of your life.

1.) It is a cash flow statement.
2.) I want to only include my own personal income (not others from shared accounts in my household).
2.b.) I want to include all expenses (including others from shared accounts in my household).

For 2. and 3. above, my reasons are, this is what I have control over. I have control over my income not others', and I have no control over no one's spending from shared accounts so i should assume the shared accounts will all be spent and the shared bills will all be paid immediately or due immedidately. 

Side note: It's a cash flow statement. That makes me a bookkeeper.

I'm a bookkeeper!

Ok so now i did 6 months so far, november to april.

Picture of wall graph here:



In conclusion, This time updating my wall graph budget, I learned it's a cash flow statement. And I learned what income and expenses I want to include on the chart, and which I want to exclude from the chart.
